Food Technology teaches students about food properties, processing, preparation, nutrition, and consumption. It emphasizes hygiene, safety, and relevant legislation. Students develop skills to produce quality food and explore how food enhances life and supports both vocational and everyday experiences.

  • Food Technology

Textiles Technology teaches students about the properties, performance, and uses of textiles, including fabrics, yarns, and fibers. They study historical, cultural, and contemporary textile design and learn to evaluate design ideas, materials, and quality. Through textile projects, students become creative, independent learners, exploring both functional and aesthetic aspects of textiles.

  • Textiles

Computing Technology 7 to 10 equips students with skills to create digital solutions in various contexts, focusing on computational, design, and systems thinking, as well as data analysis and programming. The course covers data management, cybersecurity, user experience, app development, and game creation.
Students engage with modern technologies, explore their societal impacts, and enhance their technical, project management, and problem-solving skills.
